Games, Apps and Web Portfolio

Hi, I'm Andy and I am a games programmer who makes 2D computer games that work on the web, desktop browser, mobile phone, or games for the App stores.

I use the flexible Phaser.js framework to create games, but have also extended this framework to do even more than is posible with it alone.

I have put together a showcase of some of the games, and demo games I have worked on, which give an idea of what I can do. This is the tip of the iceberg, as web games are still in their infancy, and I am exploring new ideas and projects all the time to create new interactive visuals.

I have also built Android apps using the Ionic framework and Angular.js

Biplanes Champion

A remake of the 90's single screen dog-fight game Biplane Duel. A side view of a landscape with two biplane aeroplanes that take off, perform acrobatics and duel. First to 8 points wins!

I designed the controls to mimic the tight and quick feel of the original Amiga game, even down to the engine stall if you climb to steeply.

Draw a House on a Tank game

I am making my own take on the classic turn based Tanks game. A 2D game of skill and tactics where you aim your tanks cannon and judge the shots trajectory to hit your enemy. I beefed up the idea and removed the turns, added an aiming guide, and let players "draw" they're own walls and defences to block oponents missiles.

Potential for future internet multiplay and extra weapons

Live Demo

Under the hood

Using Phaser.js, but with a custom camera routine to allow the screen to zoom out when you shoot your cannon at the distant enemy. The game has a custom destroyable bitmap landscape, and a trajectory setting joypad. Also note the bouncing bomb on level two, which calculates the surface normals on a bitmap when colliding with the landscape.

Planet Hopper

A small game demo written using the HTML5 canvas element. The rocket is controlled with the cursor keys and can explore a wrap around scrolling planet.

Space

If you venture high enough, the sky changes colour as you pass through different atmosphere layers, and eventually enter space. The game then seemlessly zooms out and you orbit the planet from space in a different game mode.

I dont currently have a demo online, but may one day convert it to Phaser and continue this game, expanding it to have missions to collect items on different planets, asteroids, underground caves and alien forests.

Asteroids

For a marketing company client I developed an Asteroids based game. ou are a rocket in space holding off a fleet of attacking flying saucers. Your rocket is controlled with the cursor keys or an onscreen joystick for mobile.

More than just graphics

Making games playable is an artform in itself, and I put thought into the way the rocket is controlled and moves to get a fun "feel".

An air friction routine was added to give the rocket punchy acceleration at low speeds, but limit maximum speed so it doesnt fly too fast, making collision courses with off screen obstacles unavoidable.
